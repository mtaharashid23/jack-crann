The Journey

A Life Devoted to Dogs

Growing up in a small beach town in Connecticut in the late 1950s and early 60s, there were dogs everywhere. There was never talk of leash laws or trainers giving group classes. It was understood you kept your dog in your yard, you walked it on a leash, and if you saw a dog tied up in someone else's yard, you didn't go near it.

I was always getting in trouble because dogs followed me home. "Whose dog is that? Where did that dog come from?" The answer was always the same: "I don't know, it followed me." I never imagined I would be spending my life learning and teaching about communicating with dogs!

In 1995, after a 20-year dance career, I applied, was accepted, and attended National K9 School for Dog Trainers in Columbus, Ohio. It was time to stop performing, and as much as I loved teaching, it was time for a change. A friend suggested dog training. I liked the idea because it was still teaching — did some research, found a school, off I went, and never looked back.

"Do what you love and love what you do. I have been blessed to do just that for over 3 decades."

My Philosophy

What I Believe

Education Over Control

The best relationship with a dog is built not through dominance, but through understanding, respect, and affection. Education is the key.

Dogs Are Family

Dogs are not disposable or temporary. We need to shift our thinking from "we have to get rid of the dog" to "we have to get educated."

Multiple Methods

There has to be more than one way to do something. To be professional, you must learn different techniques and methods for different problems and circumstances.

The Dog's Perspective

Understanding the way the dog associates is what makes your approach work. It's not words or treats or collars — it's understanding the dog's perspective.

Clarity & Reward

The dog learns from your clarity and carefully timed reward. Any tool is only as harsh as the person using it.

Rewarding Work

It is unbelievably rewarding seeing people in a happy relationship with a dog when they were certain it couldn't be done.
