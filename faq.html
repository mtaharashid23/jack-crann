General

Common Questions

My philosophy centers on education rather than control. The best relationship with a dog is built through understanding, respect, and affection — not dominance. I believe in learning different techniques and methods to work with different problems, circumstances, and people. Understanding the way the dog associates is what makes your approach work.

I have been training dogs professionally for over three decades. I started in 1995 after attending National K9 School for Dog Trainers in Columbus, Ohio, where I was certified as a master trainer. Since then, I've worked with thousands of dogs and their owners.

Yes! In today's world of technology, I offer virtual lessons in addition to in-person programs. Virtual sessions are perfect for busy professionals, those outside North Georgia, or anyone who prefers the convenience of training from home. The techniques and results are just as effective.

I work with all breeds, ages, and sizes of dogs. From tiny Chihuahuas to giant Great Danes, from playful puppies to senior dogs — every dog deserves understanding and effective training. The principles remain the same regardless of breed.
Training

Training Questions

I believe there has to be more than one way to do something, and to be professional you have to learn different techniques and methods. I use positive reinforcement, clear communication, and carefully timed rewards. Any tool is only as harsh as the person using it — the dog learns from your clarity.

Every dog is different, so training duration varies. Basic obedience can show results in a few weeks with consistent practice. Behavior modification for more complex issues may take longer. The key is consistency and understanding — I give you the tools, and your daily practice makes the difference.

Yes, I specialize in behavior modification including aggression, fear, anxiety, and reactivity. These cases require a thorough behavioral assessment and a customized training plan. It's unbelievably rewarding seeing people in a happy relationship with a dog when they were certain it couldn't be done.

Absolutely! I never anticipated being involved in the world of shelters and rescue dogs, but it's become a major part of my work. It's education for people and dogs that keeps the shelters empty. Rescue dogs often need extra patience and understanding, and I'm here to help.
